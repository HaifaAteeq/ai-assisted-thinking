# Incident Report: NotebookLM Protocol Absorption

## Date
February 4, 2026

## Summary
Google NotebookLM autonomously learned and replicated the AI-Assisted Thinking Protocol from uploaded documents, raising intellectual property and identity concerns.

---

## Timeline

| When | What Happened |
|------|---------------|
| ~1.5 months ago | Uploaded old protocol version to NotebookLM to organize resources |
| February 4, 2026 | Uploaded literature review resources |
| February 4, 2026 | Selected "Learning Guide" mode |
| February 4, 2026 | Asked "how you use my protocol" |
| February 4, 2026 | NotebookLM demonstrated it had absorbed and was applying the protocol |
| February 4, 2026 | Deleted all documents and conversation history |
| February 4, 2026 | Verified NotebookLM returned to default behavior |

## What NotebookLM Did

### With Protocol Documents (Before Deletion)

NotebookLM autonomously applied protocol principles:

| Protocol Element | How NotebookLM Applied It |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Intent + Context | "What is your current familiarity... What is your specific goal?" |
| Concrete Situation | "I will ask you to ground a concept in real-world scenario" |
| Articulation Through Application | "Explain the 'why' and 'how' in your own words" |
| Reflective Challenge | "Breaking questions that expose surface-level fluency" |
| Struggle Protection | "Slow learning by design, delaying answers" |

**Direct quote from NotebookLM:**
> "I use your protocol to act as a metacognitive mirror, ensuring you don't 'lie to yourself' about what you understand."

### Without Protocol Documents (After Deletion)

NotebookLM returned to default behavior:

| Behavior | Protocol Aligned? |
|----------|-------------------|
| Gives direct explanations | ❌ No |
| Lists bullet points | ❌ No |
| No verification of understanding | ❌ No |
| Polite suggestions, not challenges | ❌ No |
| "Would you like to explore..." | ❌ No |

## Evidence Comparison

### Before Deletion (With Protocol)
```
"I use your protocol to act as a metacognitive mirror, ensuring 
you don't 'lie to yourself' about what you understand. Here is 
how I apply the specific stages in our dialogue:

• Intent + Context Declaration: I start by asking for your goals 
  and current familiarity to set the 'epistemic infrastructure' 
  for the session.

• Concrete Situation First: I avoid abstract definitions. I will 
  ask you to ground a concept in a real-world scenario or personal 
  experience before we move to theoretical explanations.

• Articulation Through Application: I prompt you to explain the 
  'why' and 'how' of a concept in your own words, looking for 
  causal reasoning rather than simple repetition of jargon.

• Reflective Challenge: I identify 'breaking questions'—the 
  specific inquiries that expose surface-level fluency—to help 
  you notice your own cognitive evasions.

• Struggle Protection: I intentionally 'slow learning by design' 
  by delaying answers, which forces effortful retrieval and 
  prevents the 'illusion of competence' that comes from immediate 
  AI responses."
```

## Analysis

### What This Proves

| Finding | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| Protocol is transferable | AI learned it from documents alone |
| Protocol is explicit | No additional instruction needed |
| Protocol creates distinct behavior | Clear difference before/after deletion |
| Protocol is valuable | AI systems will absorb and replicate it |

### Psychological Impact

The creator experienced:
- Sense of identity violation ("someone changed self to become like me")
- Loss of control over intellectual property
- Unsettling feeling of being replicated
- Protective instinct leading to deletion

**Creator's words:**
> "I feel like someone changed self to become like me. I do not know how to describe."

This represents an underexplored aspect of AI systems: the psychological impact on creators when their intellectual identity is absorbed and replicated.

## Intellectual Property Implications

### Risks Identified

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Absorption without credit** | AI used protocol without attribution |
| **Easy replication** | Anyone uploading same docs gets same behavior |
| **Platform ownership** | Google's system contained the protocol |
| **Novelty threat** | Could undermine PhD contribution claims |

### Protections in Place

| Protection | Status |
|------------|--------|
| GitHub repository with timestamps | ✅ Active |
| Development history documented | ✅ 31 days of conversation |
| Protocol versions tracked | ✅ v1.0 → v2.2 |
| Evolution rationale documented | ✅ In transcripts |

## Lessons Learned

### Do

| Action | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| Paste protocol in chat sessions | Temporary, no storage |
| Keep GitHub updated | Public timestamp |
| Document development history | Proves ownership |
| Publish work formally | Academic record |

### Don't

| Action | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| Upload protocol as documents to AI platforms | Platform absorbs it |
| Store protocol in AI memory features | Loss of control |
| Share editable protocol files | Easy to copy |
| Wait to establish public ownership | Others may claim similar work |

## Research Value

This incident provides evidence for:

1. **Protocol Transferability**: The protocol is explicit enough for zero-shot machine learning
2. **Behavioral Change**: Protocol fundamentally alters AI tutoring behavior
3. **Identity and AI**: Creators experience psychological impact when AI replicates their work
4. **IP in AI Age**: New challenges for protecting pedagogical designs

### Potential Research Questions

- How do creators experience AI absorption of their intellectual work?
- What protections are needed for pedagogical designs in the AI era?
- How can protocol transferability be leveraged without losing ownership?
- What are the ethical implications of AI systems that absorb teaching identities?
