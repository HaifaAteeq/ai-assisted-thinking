# Research Questions
## AI-Assisted Thinking Protocol

---

## Central Research Question

> **"Can AI-assisted learning that requires demonstration of understanding (rather than passive consumption) close the gap between education and real-world application?"**

---

## Core Questions

### 1. Verification of Understanding

**RQ1:** Does requiring demonstration of understanding produce deeper learning than answer-delivery methods?

| Sub-question | Focus |
|--------------|-------|
| RQ1a | Does articulation in own words indicate real understanding? |
| RQ1b | Do transfer tests verify understanding better than recall? |
| RQ1c | Can learners apply concepts to new situations after protocol? |

---

### 2. AI Detection Capability

**RQ2:** Can AI reliably detect shallow vs. deep understanding?

| Sub-question | Focus |
|--------------|-------|
| RQ2a | What signals accurately indicate shallow understanding? |
| RQ2b | What signals accurately indicate deep understanding? |
| RQ2c | How accurate is AI detection compared to human assessment? |
| RQ2d | Can learners "game" the detection by sounding deep without understanding? |

---

### 3. Learning Persistence

**RQ3:** Does verified understanding persist longer than passive learning?

| Sub-question | Focus |
|--------------|-------|
| RQ3a | What is the retention rate at 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month? |
| RQ3b | Which types of concepts retain best/worst? |
| RQ3c | Does cross-session continuity improve long-term retention? |

### 4. Application Gap

**RQ4:** Does the protocol reduce the gap between knowing and doing?

|--------------|-------|
| RQ4a | Can learners apply learned concepts to real projects? |

## Hypotheses

Based on preliminary evidence:

**H1:** Learners who must demonstrate understanding will retain more than learners who receive explanations.

**H2:** AI can detect shallow understanding through linguistic markers (vague language, no examples, no causal reasoning).

**H3:** Learning verified through transfer tests will persist longer than learning verified through recall.

**H4:** Second-language learners benefit from protocol's language flexibility and recovery mode.

**H5:** Learners develop accurate self-assessment through repeated verification.
