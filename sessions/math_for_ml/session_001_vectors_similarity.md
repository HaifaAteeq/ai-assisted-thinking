## Learning Progression

### Concept 1: What is a Vector?

**Grounding approach used:** Google Maps / restaurant proximity

**AI question:** 
> "How do you think the computer knows which restaurants are close to you?"

**My response:** 
> "Each place has IP, group of numbers... 0s and 1s"

**AI refinement:** Connected to latitude/longitude as concrete example
- Your location: [24.7136, 46.6753]
- Restaurant A: [24.7150, 46.6760]

**Key learning moment:**
> AI: "Why does computer need TWO numbers, not one?"
> Me: "Space that need like x and y space to get some point we need two numbers"

**Verification:** ✅ Explained in own words why 2D needs 2 numbers

---

### Concept 2: Vectors Represent Anything

**AI question:** 
> "Is [Red: 255, Green: 100, Blue: 50] a vector?"

**My response:** "No"

**AI caught contradiction:**
> "You said vector is a bunch of numbers. RGB IS a bunch of numbers. Why no?"

**My clarification:**
> "I think vector is like this [255, 100, 50]" — notation issue, not concept issue

**Verification:** ✅ Understood vectors can represent colors, locations, songs—anything needing multiple numbers

---

### Concept 3: Vector Similarity

**AI setup:** Three songs as vectors
- Song A: [0.9, 0.1, 140]
- Song B: [0.8, 0.2, 135]
- Song C: [0.1, 0.9, 60]

**AI question:** 
> "Which would you recommend to someone who likes Song A?"

**My response:** "B more close"

**AI follow-up:** 
> "WHY is Song B closer?"

**My response:**
> "C is far away from other numbers in numberline or space"

**Verification:** ✅ Articulated similarity = closeness of numbers
